Register

The user signs up as a Seller (like restaurants or individuals with surplus food) or as a Buyer (like NGOs, biogas plants, or households). During registration, details such as name, category, email, and password are securely stored in MongoDB.

Login

The registered user logs in by selecting their role: Seller, Buyer, or Admin. Flask handles authentication and redirects users to their respective dashboards. Admin has control over user verification and monitoring activity.

Post Food

Sellers can access a food posting form where they enter details such as food type (veg/non-veg), quantity, restaurant/vendor name, and location. These entries are stored in MongoDB and displayed to buyers based on matching location or category.

Browse Listings

Buyers (NGOs, plants, households) can enter a location to browse posted food cards. Flask queries MongoDB to fetch all matching posts and display them with information like food type, location, and upload time. This helps buyers act quickly before food expires.

Place Order

If a buyer is interested in a food post, they can accept the offer. The system records this action and notifies the seller. Flask updates the post status and optionally logs feedback or interest from multiple buyers for analytics.

Collect or Deliver

Once a post is accepted, the food is either picked up by the buyer (e.g., NGO team or a biogas collector) or delivered if logistics are managed. The collection status is updated in the backend, and a confirmation is stored to mark the cycle complete.
